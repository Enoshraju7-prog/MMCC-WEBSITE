# Blog Writing Process — Run This Every Time

Before writing a single word, do all 8 steps below. No skipping.

---

## Step 1 — Search Google for the primary keyword

Use WebSearch with the exact target keyword (e.g. "car dent repair kakinada" or "car service checklist india").

## Step 2 — Fetch and read the top 3 ranking pages

Use WebFetch on the top 3 URLs from the search results. Read the full content of each page.

## Step 3 — Match their format

What format are the top 3 using?
- Listicle (5 tips, 7 signs, 10 steps)
- Tutorial (how to do X step by step)
- Guide (complete explainer)
- Comparison (X vs Y)

Match the dominant format of the top 3. If 2 of 3 are listicles, write a listicle.

## Step 4 — Match their length (within 20%)

Count the approximate word count of each top 3 article. Take the average. Write within 20% of that average.

Example: top 3 are 900, 1100, 800 words → average = 933 → target 750–1120 words.

## Step 5 — Cover every topic the top 3 all talk about

List every H2/H3 heading across all 3 pages. Any topic that appears in 2 or more of the top 3 — include it. These are the topics Google expects to see to rank.

## Step 6 — Add 1–2 extra topics they missed

Find something genuinely useful that none of the top 3 covered. This is MM Car Care's edge. Good sources:
- Kakinada-specific angle (coastal climate, monsoon roads, summer heat)
- A real workshop insight that generic sites wouldn't have
- A FAQ question from "People Also Ask" that the top 3 ignored

## Step 7 — Answer the main question directly at the top

The very first paragraph should answer the primary keyword question in 2–3 sentences. This is what Google pulls for featured snippets. Don't bury the answer — lead with it, then explain.

Example for "car service checklist india":
> "A standard car service in India covers engine oil, oil filter, air filter, cabin filter, tyre inspection, brake check, fluid top-ups, and battery test. Most cars need a basic service every 5,000–10,000 km. Here's the full checklist and what each item actually involves."

## Step 8 — Include an FAQ section at the end

Search "People Also Ask" results from Step 1 and pick the 3–5 most relevant questions. Answer each in 2–4 sentences. This section:
- Earns FAQ rich snippets in Google
- Captures long-tail keyword traffic
- Keeps the reader on the page longer

## Image Rule

Use the Pexels API (`/api/pexels`) to find one relevant image per major section (above H2s where possible). Each image needs a `pexelsQuery` — specific beats generic. "car mechanic checking engine oil india" beats "car service."
